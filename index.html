Welcome

Research within the numerical mathematics group at KAUST focuses on the design, analysis, and implementation of numerical methods for ordinary and partial differential equations, as well as application of numerical methods primarily to problems in nonlinear wave propagation. Our work spans the numerical method development pipeline from abstract mathematical analysis, to testing, to software development and computational science, in collaboration with application domain researchers. Particular emphasis is placed on robustness, wide applicability, and efficiency.

Software

We develop the freely available numerical package PetClaw for solving nonlinear hyperbolic PDEs on massively parallel computers.

Open positions

We have openings for postdoctoral fellows in the areas of numerical analysis, scientific software development, and computational modeling of nonlinear waves.
